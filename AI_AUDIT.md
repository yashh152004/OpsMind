# OpsMind: AI & Intelligence Audit

Analysis of the autonomous reasoning layer.

## 1. AI Components
### AI SRE Copilot (Chat)
- **Status**: **FULLY WORKING**
- **Logic**: POST `/api/ai/chat`.
- **Function**: Handles free-text queries, incident analysis commands (`/analyze`), and general SRE questions.

### AI Insights HUD (Dashboard)
- **Status**: **FULLY WORKING (Simulated/Real-time Mix)**
- **Logic**: GET `/api/ai/insights`.
- **Function**: Displays "Intelligence Reasoning" rows with confidence scores.

## 2. Technical Implementation
- **Prompt Flow**: Managed by the `ai-engine` (Python) or a specialized Spring Boot service.
- **Context Sources**:
    - **Current Alert**: Passed during `/analyze alert {id}`.
    - **Incident Context**: Severity, title, and affected service.
    - **Inventory**: Node metadata (Type, Region).
- **Reasoning Sources**: LLM chains (likely Vertex AI / Gemini via the shared backend).

## 3. Findings & Limitations
- **Latency**: Large reasoning chains can take 5+ seconds to resolve without a streaming UI.
- **Knowledge Base**: Currently lacks access to external documentation (e.g., standard SRE runbooks) beyond its training data.
- **Verification**: Confidence scores are generated by the model but not backed by a second "verifier" chain.
- **Actionability**: The AI can "recommend" actions but cannot currently "execute" them (no /fix command).

## 4. Workflows
1. **Analyze Incident**: User clicks AI icon on an incident row -> Chat opens with pre-filled `/analyze incident 123`.
2. **Predictive Analytics**: Insights page highlights potential future failures based on trend analysis.
